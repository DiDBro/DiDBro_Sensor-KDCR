#### 块 1.1：The Challenge（约 15cm 高）

**标题**：`The Challenge`（深蓝底白字小标题）

**正文**（直接复制粘贴）：
```
Traditional precision control of magnetic levitation relies on 
expensive sensors (laser displacement, force gauges). Using low-cost 
industrial cameras introduces critical challenges:

• Frame-rate loss and processing latency (~13–27 ms actual vs. 
  10 ms target at 100 Hz)
• Severe non-linearity in electromagnetic forces — no off-the-shelf 
  formula exists
• Mechanical unpredictability: random multidirectional friction in 
  conventional mechanical linkages makes 1D vertical motion unrepeatable
```

#### 块 1.2：Our Objective（约 12cm 高）

**标题**：`Our Objective`

**正文**：
```
1. Upgrade mechanical design for pure 1D motion — replace acrylic 
   tube with precision glass rod through the ball's center hole.
   Eliminating parasitic lateral oscillations revealed the true 
   damping mechanism: Coulomb dry friction.

2. Eliminate camera frame-drop via Raspberry Pi parallel processing 
   (software-triggered 100 Hz stable capture with millimeter-level 
   ball centroid detection).

3. Develop accurate mathematical models of nonlinear electromagnetic 
   and friction forces, laying the foundation for future closed-loop 
   control.

4. Systematically identify system parameters (stiffness k, friction 
   force Ff, natural frequency fn, damping mechanism) through 
   cross-validated free-oscillation analysis of 30 datasets.
```

### 🔵 第2列 — Electromagnetic Force Modeling

#### 块 2.1：Physical Model（约 10cm 高）

**标题**：`EM Force: Variable Separation Hypothesis`

**核心公式**（放最大字号，选框高亮）：
$$\frac{F_{EM}}{M_b g} = f(V) \cdot f(z) = \left[ A \left(\frac{V}{V_{max}}\right)^2 + B \left(\frac{V}{V_{max}}\right) \right] \left(\frac{z}{D_b}\right)^C$$

**公式下方的小字说明**：
```
Experimental basis: 2 ball sizes (M12, M15) × 6 voltages (4–24V) 
× 7 distances (2–20 mm) — total 400+ force measurements
using a precision force gauge.
```

---

#### 块 2.2：Two-Step Fitting Method（约 15cm 高）

**标题**：`Two-Step Dimensionless Fitting`

**放一张流程示意图**（自己做或用 nanobanana 生成）：

```
Step 1: Strip Geometry → Extract Pure Electrical Term f(V)
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ For each voltage, assume global exponent C is known    │
│ f(V)_measured = Y_F / (X_z)^C                        │
│ Average all points at same V → robust K_v per voltage │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                         ↓
Step 2: Linearize → Fit (A, B) with 1st-Order Polynomial
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Model:  f(V) = A(V/Vmax)² + B(V/Vmax)                │
│ Let x = V/Vmax, divide both sides by x:               │
│ f(V)/x = Ax + B    ← a straight line!                │
│ Simple polyfit — avoids nonlinear optimization        │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

#### 块 3.2：Damping Mechanism — The Coulomb Discovery（约 12cm 高）

**标题**：`Damping Mechanism Identification`

**放一张峰值衰减图**（从 `May_18/fig_46_coulomb_damping.png` 或 `fig_62_peak_decay.png` 复制）

**对比表**：
| Damping Model | Decay Law | Mean R² Across 30 Trials |
|--------------|-----------|-------------------------|
| **Coulomb (Linear)** ★ | Aₙ = A₀ − n·ΔA | **0.996 – 0.999** |
| Viscous (Exponential) | Aₙ = A₀·e^(−nδ) | 0.75 – 0.95 |

**高亮结论框**（绿色或蓝色边框）：
```
🔑 Coulomb dry friction dominates.
Per-cycle amplitude decay ≈ 0.85 mm/cycle — constant and 
independent of amplitude. This is the hallmark signature of 
dry friction, not viscous damping.

Physical interpretation: friction originates from dynamic side loads 
between the through-hole and glass rod — NOT from gravity (Mg = 62.6 mN 
would be an order of magnitude larger than the observed Ff ≈ 2.2 mN).
```

#### 块 3.5：Uncertainty Quantification（约 8cm 高）

**标题**：`How Many Cycles Are Enough?`

```
Jackknife resample analysis reveals that parameter uncertainty 
scales inversely with the number of detectable oscillation cycles:

  5 mm height (~5 peaks) → stiffness 95% CI: ±57%
 15 mm height (~15 peaks) → stiffness 95% CI: ±25%

Practical guideline: ≥10 detectable cycles needed for stiffness 
uncertainty below ±30%.

The half-split test (first vs. last 50% of peaks) shows no 
systematic bias → Coulomb model applies uniformly throughout 
the entire decay.
```
